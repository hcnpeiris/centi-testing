Chapter One

The Beginning

It began with four ordinary college students bound by a single ambition — to make music that stood apart.

They opened with covers drawn from the Pop, Ethnic RnB, and Alternative catalogues, and even as newcomers they won over every crowd they played to. In 2002 they stepped into the studio to record two original singles — "Tradition" and "Dark Angel" — built for high-profile gatherings and college conferences. Both up-tempo tracks became instant hits, catching the ear of the country's leading media networks and picking up nationwide airplay through 2003.

Chapter Two

Rise to Fame

"Tradition" debuted on the Sri Lankan Hot 40 chart at #2. "Dark Angel" climbed into ABC Network's Sun FM Hot 40 Countdown within 24 hours — and by the following week it had claimed #1, outranking major international acts and holding the top spot for four straight weeks. Both tracks were even picked up for airplay on BBC Radio 1.

Their 2005 debut album "Heritage" shot up the charts and still holds the record for the fastest-selling debut album in Sri Lanka. CENTIGRADZ followed with a run of groundbreaking music videos — "Pahasara," "Oba Ekwenawado," and "Ranwan Mal Dam" — dominating the video charts for 15 consecutive weeks in 2008. They also wrote the theme songs for the films "Heart FM" and "Asai Man Piyabanna."

Chapter Three
World Stage

The World Stage

CENTIGRADZ were chosen to represent Sri Lanka at the "Asia Pacific World Star Quest" Finals in Penang, Malaysia — a competition drawing acts from 13 countries. Their set at the open-air Auto City concert carried the kind of poise and professionalism that earned them the "Excellent New Singer Award."

They followed it up with back-to-back "Most Popular Music Group of the Year" wins, and took home the State Presidential Music Award for "Best Melody Composition" for their smash hit "Diurala Pawasanna." With over 500 sold-out shows behind them, the band still draws the country's highest ticket sales — powered by fans who keep coming back for their unity, simplicity, and undimmed passion for music.
